Learning Symmetrical Cross-Modal Correlations for Speech-Preserving Facial Expression Manipulation

1Guangdong University of Technology    2South China University of Technology
3Sun Yat-sen University

Abstract

Speech-preserving facial expression manipulation (SPFEM) aims to automatically modify facial emotions while maintaining facial animations of the speech content. Current works depend on inaccessible paired training examples for each person, where two corresponding frames represent identical speech content but with the source and target emotions for each person, rendering SPFEM impractical in real-world applications. Fortunately, it is inherent that similar speeches are highly correlated with corresponding facial expressions and mouth movements. In this work, we propose a novel symmetrical cross-modal correlation learning (SCMCL) algorithm that investigates this characteristic to construct paired supervision to improve facial expression manipulation and meanwhile better preserve the facial animation of speech content. Specifically, it first learns a symmetrical cross-modal correlation metric, ensuring that the similarity in one modality (e.g., audio) enjoys a certain correlation with that in another modality (e.g., images). During training, it retrieves a video clip, which is recorded in the target emotion and its audio clip is highly similar to the input audio clip. It then uses the learned correlations between the audio similarity of the given and retrieved audio clips and the visual similarity of the given and generated visual information as an additional objective to supervise generating visual content. The objective is a plug-and-play term that can be effortlessly integrated with existing SPEFM algorithms to facilitate high-quality facial expression manipulation. Our extensive qualitative and quantitative evaluations across various settings demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.
